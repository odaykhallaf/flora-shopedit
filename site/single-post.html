Research title: Medicinal and therapeutic properties of the Palestinian thyme plant

Palestinian thyme is considered one of the important aromatic and medicinal plants that flourish in the Palestinian territories. Thyme has been used since ancient times in traditional and folk medicine thanks to its unique healing properties. Thyme contains volatile oils rich in phenolic compounds, such as thymol and carvacol, which have antibacterial and antifungal properties. In addition, thyme is a rich source of antioxidants that help protect the body from damage caused by free radicals. Thyme is also used to relieve respiratory and digestive problems, making it a plant of great medicinal importance in Palestinian and international heritage.

Thyme contains volatile oils rich in phenolic compounds such as thymol and carvacol, and these compounds possess strong antibacterial and antifungal properties. These properties make thyme an effective treatment for a wide range of inflammation and infections. In addition, thyme is a rich source of antioxidants that contribute to protecting the body's cells from damage caused by free radicals, and thus plays an important role in preventing chronic diseases such as cancer and heart disease.

Palestinian thyme is also used to relieve respiratory problems such as coughing and asthma, as it helps calm the airways and relieve congestion. Regarding the digestive system, thyme is useful in treating stomach and intestinal disorders, including gas and intestinal cramps. Thanks to these multiple health benefits, Palestinian thyme remains an important medicinal plant in Palestinian heritage, where it is included in many traditional medicinal recipes. With modern scientific advances, research continues to discover more health benefits of the thyme plant, strengthening its position as an effective and safe natural remedy for use in many health conditions.
